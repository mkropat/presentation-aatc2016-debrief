James Lewis

Microservices and Conway's Law

“organizations which design systems ... are constrained to produce designs which are copies of the communication structures of these organizations”

— Melvin Conway (1967)

The Idea

Coarse-grained communication between teams leads to coarse-grained APIs between teams

Teams that are under different org structures

Teams that are physical separate

Fine-grained communication between people leads to fine-grained (RPC-style) APIs

Teams that sit next to each other

Or a single team that owns many different microservices

RPC-style APIs between teams

Specific API contracts outlining specific request payloads and responses

Tends to require coordinated releases

Fan-in for end-to-end testing

Easy to abuse responsibilities of different services

Can lead to worst of both worlds...
a distributed monolith

Different Styles Of Pairing

  1. Traditional Driver-Navigator

    Driver — types code and is in charge of the details

    Navigator — asks high level questions and watches for mistakes

  2. Hybrid Pairing

    Driver — in charge of the details and figures out what to do next

    Typer — the only person who's allowed to touch the keyboard
